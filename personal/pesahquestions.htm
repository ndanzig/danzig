36. Puah was Miriam and was called that because she could quiet the babies by singing to them. Shifra was Yocheved (Miriam's mother) and she was called this because she cleaned the babies up.

37. The shotrim were the Jewish bosses over the slave Jews. The nogshim were the Egyptian bosses over the Jewish bosses (Rashi).

38.  The third (lice), sixth (shchin - boils), ninth(darkness) and the first born.  It seems that the principle of hazaka is in play here since always the third plague is without warning.  And then the third of the three.

39. It is forbidden by Jewish custom to eat matzah from 30 days before Pesach , i.e. Purim.

40. It is forbidden to eat hametz according to Torah law from noon on Pesach eve. But rabbinically it is forbidden two hours before that.

41. Because it is written in Shmot 15:26 (right after Red Sea crossing) that all the illnesses of Egypt G-d will save the Israelites from. So, the more illnesses there were, the more we will be saved from!

43. Verse 7:9 calls the staff to snake trick a mofet.

44. The miracle of the staffs turning to snakes was done with their lahatehem (with a heh), the plague of blood (1) was recreated (without a heh), frogs (2) (without heh), and they tried to recreate the lice (3) (without a heh) but failed.
