Baud Rate
•The rate of transfer is in BPS. The UART 8250 has a clock that's rate is 1.8432 MHz. The UART divides the clock signal by 16, giving a maximum baud of 115,200 BPS. •But this rate might be too fast for some devices, so the rate is controllable. •Lets say we want to communicate at 2400 BPS. We have to divide the clock rate by a number multiplied by 16 to get the desired rate. This number is called the clock divisor.
•Thus, in our case 2400 = 1.8432*106/(clock divisor * 16).
•The clock divisor = 1.8432*106/(2400 * 16) = 48
•The clock divisor is stored in the DLL and DLM before transmission starts. The DLL Contains the 8 LSBs (Least Significant Bits) and the DLM contains the 8 MSBs (Most Significant Bits).
